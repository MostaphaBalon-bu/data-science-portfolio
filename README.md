## **Project Title: The Gender Cancer Divide**
 **Overview**

This project analyzes global cancer mortality data to understand how cancer affects men and women differently. Using data from the Global Burden of Disease (GBD) Study, the analysis focuses on long-term mortality trends, risk factors, and disparities in survival rates.

 **Problem Statement**

Cancer is often discussed in general terms, but mortality patterns differ significantly by sex. This project investigates:

Which cancers contribute most to global deaths
Why lung cancer disproportionately affects men
How much of the cancer burden is preventable

 **Data Sources**
 
Our World in Data (2022)
IHME Global Burden of Disease Study 2019

 **Methods**
 
Data cleaning and preprocessing using pandas
Descriptive statistical analysis
Time series analysis (1950–2020)
Comparative analysis by sex
Data visualization with matplotlib

 **Key Findings**
 
Male-predominant cancers account for over 50% of global cancer deaths
Lung cancer is the leading cause of cancer mortality worldwide
~25% of cancer deaths are attributable to tobacco use
Survival rates vary significantly depending on early detection
